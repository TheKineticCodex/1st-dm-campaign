# THE FIRST ACT PROMPT
### The directive for designing Act 1 of "The Song the Sea Forgot" — complete, branch-proof, and battle-ready
*Companion to `story-bible.md` (the world's truth) and `PROMPT-V2.md` (the app's law). This document governs how Act 1 gets designed. Execute it and the output replaces guesswork with a playbook: every scene, every fight that must happen, every fight that might, every reward, every level-up, every app cue — so the DM never improvises structure, only performance.*

---

## 0. The one sentence

Design Act 1 so completely that on game night the DM holds a map of **everything the table could do** — and whichever way four brand-new players jump, the ground is already load-bearing.

## 1. Inputs (all exist — use them, never contradict them)

1. **The story bible** — the Still Court, the first bargain and its flaw, the carnival, the Three Gates, the cast (Twins of the Toll, Grandmother Grey-Gill, the Buyer).
2. **The real party** (from the live Vault; refresh before designing): Peaches capiche (Aasimar Druid — mermaid, Lost Thing: her tail; fear: her alligator) · William "Blue-Chew Billy" Blackwood (Human Wizard — showman, Lost Thing: mentor's spellbook; fear: the mask coming off) · Philip (undivined — leave a woven slot) · Player 4 (unjoined — leave a woven slot). Slots must be first-class: scenes reference "the third traveler's thread" with connection points ready to bind the moment their confessions land.
3. **The app's instruments** — every scene must name its cues: sealed whispers ✉, contracts ⚖, the Snail Derby 🐌, conditions/concentration, the dice feed, the stage 🎭 (which map is showing), and the timeline beat it belongs to.
4. **The rules floor** — 2024 rules; SRD 5.2 (CC-BY-4.0) creatures ONLY, referenced by name with page-free stat summaries; never reproduce non-SRD text; house adaptations get `// VERIFY` and a line in the summary.

## 2. The leveling law (milestone — no XP bookkeeping, ever)

- **Level 1 → 2: end of Session 1**, the moment the party steps through whichever Gate they choose. Crossing the threshold IS the milestone; the Feywild notices. (App: the level-up lands before Session 2 so sheets are ready — the party fights nothing deadlier than a Hard encounter while level 1.)
- **Level 2 → 3: the midpoint of their chosen Act 2 road** — specifically, the scene where they first **cost the Still Court something real** (free a jarred voice, crack a mirror, void a market contract). Power as a consequence of defiance.
- **Level 3 at Act 1's true end** (the road's climax, entering Act 2 proper) — subclasses unlock exactly when the story widens. Design the climax to *earn* it.
- Constraint on all combat design: at level 1, 4 PCs with ~9–11 HP each; **no single foe whose max damage one-shots a PC from full**; save-or-suffer effects get warning beats first. At level 2+, loosen accordingly.
- `// VERIFY` every encounter-budget claim against the 2024 DMG encounter math (Low/Moderate/High XP budgets for 4 PCs at levels 1–3).

## 3. What must be designed (the deliverable's table of contents)

For **every beat** already in the timeline (Gate → Midway → Grey-Gill → Missing Note → Whispers → Three Gates) **plus each of the three roads' first three scenes** (Bog / Facets / Under-Sea — yes, all three: the unchosen must be as real as the chosen):

**A. The scene ledger** — one page per scene:
- *Open* — a 2–4 sentence read-aloud in the campaign voice (original prose).
- *Truth* — what is actually going on (DM only, one paragraph).
- *The five doors* — what happens if the party: fights · talks · sneaks · bargains · does something insane. Every scene answers all five. "Combat is optional but always possible" is the standing rule.
- *App cues* — exact instrument and moment (e.g., "when she takes Peaches' hand → ✉ whisper #3 to Peaches only").
- *Exits* — which beats this scene can lead to, including sideways.

**B. The battle book:**
- **Major battles (2 per road, 1 possible at the carnival):** each with — SRD creature list with role labels (brute/skirmisher/controller), the encounter-budget math shown, the arena described for a stage map (what the iPad shows; what terrain players can *use*), 3 environmental tricks, the foe's goal (never "kill the party" — take, delay, repossess), the retreat/parley condition, and what changes in the story on win, loss, and draw. **A loss is a story turn, not a TPK** — the Court *collects*, it doesn't kill; design every loss as "something is taken."
- **Minor battle opportunities (3+ per road, 2 at the carnival):** small, optional, triggerable — each one line of trigger ("if they pry open the jar wagon…"), one line of force ("2 SRD X + 1 SRD Y"), one line of prize/cost. These are the DM's pressure valves for a table that wants to swing swords.
- **The alligator rule:** Peaches' alligator is present, uncombatant, and *load-bearing* — every major battle notes where it is and what it does (warn, drag someone out, stare at the water). It can be endangered, never killed.

**C. The reward ledger** — every treasure, prize, and boon in Act 1: mundane loot (the module-shaped hole: enough gold to matter, somewhere to spend it — the Bog Merchant), 3–5 minor wondrous items (original, fairy-tale, one per PC thread), each character's **first Reliquary fragment** (one per road, placed at the road's midpoint milestone), and the carnival prizes (paper crown, bottled applause) with their secret uses.

**D. The what-if appendix** — the twenty most likely table-breakers, each with a one-paragraph answer: they attack the Twins · they refuse all three gates · they split the party across two gates · they try to steal the carousel's organ · Billy signs something terrible immediately · Peaches announces the truth to everyone · they befriend the Buyer · they try to leave the carnival the way they came · a player is absent · a player dies anyway (the Court offers a bargain at the moment of death — death is a *negotiation*).

**E. The session skeletons** — Act 1 sliced into 3–4 sessions with per-session: opening recap cue, expected beats, the cliff to end on, and the level-up ceremony placement.

## 4. Design laws

1. **Branch equality.** The three roads get identical craft. The table must never sense which road was "the real one."
2. **The three-clue rule everywhere.** Any conclusion the plot needs has three routes to it; every mandatory revelation appears in at least two roads.
3. **Confessions are canon.** The players' own vault words appear, verbatim or nearly, at least once per session — through the ✉ callback engine.
4. **The Court buys; it never robs.** Every antagonist action is a transaction someone technically agreed to. Every fight is a repossession, an enforcement, or a guard dog.
5. **Original prose only.** Fairy-tale register, the lanterns' voice for anything players hear; SRD names for stat blocks are the only borrowed words.
6. **Everything lands in the app.** The output isn't a document the DM reads at the table — it seeds `story_nodes` (scenes and branches), NPC cards, the clue tracker, and stage maps. The document is the source; the Book is the instrument.

## 5. Output format

A single `campaign/act1-playbook.md` ordered: Session skeletons → Scene ledgers → Battle book → Reward ledger → What-if appendix → a final **seed manifest** (the exact story_nodes/NPCs/clues to add, so the app can be updated mechanically). Every `// VERIFY` collected in a closing list.

## 6. Definition of done

The DM can be asked any question that begins "what happens if they—" about Act 1, and the answer is already written down. That, and four level-up moments that feel like the Feywild itself opened a door.

*Design it like the tide made it: patient, inevitable, and with three ways into every cove.*
