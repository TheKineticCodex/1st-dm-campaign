# PART I — SESSION SKELETONS

**Session 1 — "The Night of Lanterns"** (carnival, beats 1–6)
Open: ambient mode on the iPad as they sit down; character creation for stragglers is the cold open (the divination IS the ritual). Beats: Gate → Midway (🐌 Derby) → Grey-Gill → Missing Note → Whispers ✉ → Three Gates. **Level 2 the instant they cross their Gate** — end on the crossing itself: one sentence of what the light does to them, then black. *Cliff: do not show them the other side.*

**Session 2 — the chosen road, scenes 1–2**
Open: "Previously" (3 bullets) + the road's arrival read-aloud. Scene 1 (travel + Major Battle A likely) → Scene 2 (the bargainer's house — talk, deal, steal). **Level 3 at the midpoint milestone** — the moment they *cost the Court something real* (free a voice / crack a mirror / void a sale). *Cliff: the Court's response is announced — the Tithe Barge horn / opening-night invitations / the auction bell.*

**Session 3 — the chosen road, scene 3 (climax)**
Major Battle B, the road's setpiece. Win, lose, or draw, Act 1 ends with the first Reliquary fragment secured (or its location burning in their minds) and the Buyer's silhouette seen for the first time. *Cliff into Act 2: the receipt — whatever they saved, something ELSE on it was already sold.*

**Session 4 (buffer)** — if pacing runs long, split Session 2 after its Major Battle A. Never rush the bargainer scene; it's the campaign in miniature.

# PART II — SCENE LEDGERS

## THE CARNIVAL

### C1 · The Gate of Paper Lanterns
**Open:** *"The tide went out this evening and forgot to come back. Where it should be stands a fence of paper lanterns, and a gate, and two figures — one humming half a song, one holding a slate that says WELCOME in wet ink."*
**Truth:** The Twins' toll ("one honest answer") is Court paperwork — consent, witnessed, filed. Keep every answer in your session notes verbatim.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* the Twins don't resist; the lanterns dim one by one until the party stops (no stats — the carnival simply goes dark around violence; shame beats swords). *Talk:* the toll, warmly. *Sneak:* over the fence — they arrive inside marked: every lantern turns to look at them all night (disadvantage on Stealth inside `// VERIFY house rule`). *Bargain:* Hush will accept a secret instead of an answer, ⚖ contract, price: "one secret, of Our choosing, later." *Insane:* see What-Ifs 1, 8.
**App cues:** stage 🎭 = carnival map · this beat = timeline "The Gate of Paper Lanterns."
**Exits:** → C2, or anywhere on the midway.

### C2 · The Games of the Midway
**Open:** *"Everything glitters, nothing has a price tag, and every stall-keeper knows your name before you say it."*
**Truth:** Prizes are skimmed things. The prize stall pays Billy in a page of his mentor's handwriting — the stallkeep genuinely doesn't know its worth ("things wash up").
**Five doors:** *Fight:* → Minor Battles m1/m2, or interfere with the Empty Costumes → Major Battle CARN-A. *Talk:* stallkeeps gossip: "the Toll-Twins never sleep," "don't hum near the carousel." *Sneak:* behind the prize stall — crates addressed in salt-crusted labels to "THE STILL COURT, BELOW." First written clue. *Bargain:* anything can be bought here — for teeth, memories, birthdays (⚖ quill for anything serious). *Insane:* What-Ifs 4, 13, 14.
**App cues:** 🐌 **run the Snail Derby here** — winner takes the Paper Crown; runner-up the Bottled Applause. Billy's page: hand him a real folded note at the table if you can.
**Exits:** → C3 (Grey-Gill beckons the Derby winner personally) or C4.

### C3 · Grandmother Grey-Gill's Tent
**Open:** *"The tent smells of salt and low tide. The woman inside has a shawl pinned high at the neck, and eyes like the last hour before a storm."*
**Truth:** She sold her name; she cannot speak the Court's, literally — when she tries, only bubbles come. Demonstrate this once. She recognizes Peaches instantly.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* she yields immediately — and the tent itself weeps salt water until they leave; nothing gained, one fortune lost forever. *Talk:* her readings = their real vault answers (✉ forge them). To Peaches: *"You walked in on borrowed feet, child. The sea still sets a place for you at supper."* *Sneak:* her lockbox holds a receipt: "RECEIVED: one name (GREY-GILL). PAID: one tent, waterproof, eternal." — the Court's paper, second written clue. *Bargain:* she'll trade a true answer for a kindness done on whatever road they take (a hook per road, below). *Insane:* What-If 12.
**App cues:** ✉ one whisper per PC during the readings — their own confessions as prophecy.
**Exits:** → C4 (she flinches when the carousel music drifts in — point it out).

### C4 · The Missing Note
**Open:** *"The carousel turns. The organ plays a sea-song. And every few bars — a hole, the same hole, where a note should be. The horses' glass eyes are all turned toward the water."*
**Truth:** The organ was built around the Sea's stolen song; the missing note is the one the Court could not buy — because it belongs to Peaches.
**Five doors:** *Fight/steal the organ:* What-If 4 (Major CARN-A triggers). *Talk:* the operator hums along and weeps without noticing. *Sneak:* inside the organ, a brass cylinder etched: "PROPERTY OF THE STILL COURT — TRANSCRIPTION INCOMPLETE." Third written clue (three-clue rule satisfied on "the Court exists and owns this place's heart"). *Bargain:* the operator will stop the ride for a song sung live — if Peaches sings, every lantern in the carnival turns toward her (goosebumps beat; the Court now knows she's here). *Insane:* if anyone HUMS the missing note — the carnival holds its breath for three full seconds. Do this with real table silence.
**Exits:** → C5 (dusk falls, lanterns dim for the midnight show).

### C5 · Whispers in the Dark
**Open:** *"The lanterns dim for the midnight show. In the dark between them, your glass grows warm."*
**Truth:** The carnival's proximity to the Court thins the veil; their Lost Things reach for them.
**Five doors:** this is a delivery beat, not a challenge — ✉ each PC: Peaches: *wet footprints ending mid-stride*; Billy: *ink drying on a page no hand writes*; Philip/P4: woven from their vault the moment it lands. *If they chase the visions:* each vision walks them to the back fence. All doors lead to C6.
**App cues:** the four whispers, 60-second ephemeral mode ON (dreams don't keep).

### C6 · The Three Gates
**Open:** *"The back fence isn't a fence anymore. It's three gates. Green lanterns, and the smell of moss. Silver lanterns, and your own face looking back. Blue lanterns, and stairs going down into water that waits."*
**Truth:** All three are real, all three are prepared, and the Court watches which they choose (the unchosen "remember").
**Five doors:** *Fight:* nothing to fight but the choice. *Talk:* Hush's slate: "ONE OPENS TONIGHT. THE OTHERS REMEMBER BEING CHOSEN LAST." *Sneak/split/refuse:* What-Ifs 2, 3, 18. *Bargain:* Hum will hint — for one memory of the carnival (they forget one beat of tonight; you choose which and tell that player privately ✉).
**LEVEL 2 fires when they cross.** Read: *"The Feywild takes notice of you"* — let the phones do the ceremony at next open.
**Exits:** ⑂ Bog (B1) · ⑂ Facets (M1) · ⑂ Under-Sea (U1).

## ROAD A — THE DROWNED BOG

### B1 · The Whispering Causeway
**Open:** *"A road of floating lanterns crosses black water. The water is full of voices. They are all very polite, and they all want something."*
**Truth:** The voices are Cassia's inventory, leaking. They beg to be hummed, spoken, carried.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* Minors b-m1/b-m2/b-m3 trigger here (below). *Talk (to the voices):* one voice knows the missing note's story — "the sea sold the song, but a mermaid was born holding one note of it, after the sale." Clue route 2 for the finale-flaw. *Sneak:* off-causeway wading = Crocodile (b-m2) + everything smells them. *Bargain:* a voice offers to guide them for a ride in someone's mouth (creepy, harmless, hilarious — until it screams at the wrong moment: use it as a one-time alarm the DM controls). *Insane:* drinking bog water: 1 level exhaustion `// VERIFY` and one true dream of the Under-Sea.
**Major Battle A (BOG-A) usually triggers at the causeway's end.**
**Exits:** → B2.

### B2 · Cassia's Jar-House *(midpoint · LEVEL 3 milestone)*
**Open:** *"A house on stilts wearing other houses' shutters. Inside, shelves to the ceiling: green jars, each humming, each labeled in a shaking hand."*
**Truth:** Old Cassia (social encounter, not a fight — stat as Green Hag ONLY if it goes wrong `// VERIFY SRD`) is drowning in tithe-debt. She hates the Court and fears it more. She keeps ONE jar she refuses to sell: her sister's laugh.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* she defends with the shelves — every broken jar frees a Shadow (b-m4). Winning gains jars but loses the guide who knows the Barge's schedule. *Talk:* she'll name the Buyer's *route* (never the name) if they take her sister's laugh somewhere safe — Grey-Gill's kindness-hook pays off here. *Sneak:* the ledger under her mattress lists: "ONE NOTE OF THE SEA'S SONG — jarred, awaiting collection" — **the fragment, in the room with them.** *Bargain:* ⚖ quill — she'll trade the note-jar for the party assuming ONE tithe payment ("something precious, intangible, delivered when the Barge comes"). Read the fine print at the table; it does not say whose. *Insane:* opening the note-jar early: the note sings itself home to Peaches — she carries it audibly forever after (beautiful; also a tracking beacon — the Court can always find her now).
**MILESTONE:** freeing any voice (the sister's laugh, the note, any jar released with intent) = **Level 3.** The bog goes silent for one full minute — the first silence in centuries — then every voice left whispers *thank you.*
**Exits:** → B3 (the Barge horn sounds across the water — end Session 2 here).

### B3 · The Tithe Barge *(climax — Major Battle BOG-B)*
**Open:** *"It arrives without a wake. A barge of grey wood, stacked with crates that hum, poled by something wearing a ferryman's coat, and the coat is the most alive thing about it."*
See Battle Book. Win: the Barge's **manifest** — proof the Court's purchases route "BELOW, VIA THE MARKET" (Act 2 destination unlocked) — plus the note-jar if not already taken. Loss: the Court collects one voice from a PC (player speaks only in whispers until recovered in Act 2 — collected, never killed). Draw/flee: the Barge departs on schedule; the manifest's carbon-copy is findable in Cassia's debt-papers (three-clue redundancy).

## ROAD B — THE HALL OF FACETS

### M1 · The Gallery of Best Faces
**Open:** *"A palace of standing mirrors with no walls holding them up. None of them show you. All of them show someone's finest hour, on loop."*
**Truth:** Bought performances, displayed. One mirror plays Billy's mentor mid-laugh — mid-lesson — mid-life. Another stands covered; under the sheet, Billy with no smile at all ("acquisition pending").
**Five doors:** *Fight:* Minors m-m1/m-m2/m-m3. *Talk (to mirrors):* they answer with their one moment only — but ORDER of moments can spell messages; the mentor's mirrors, watched in sequence, teach Billy a spell's missing gesture (give him a free casting of a 1st-level spell once `// VERIFY house rule`). *Sneak:* the Curator's catalog: the mentor's set is "INCOMPLETE — FINAL VOLUME (THE JOURNAL) IN TRANSIT." His spellbook is HERE, in transit — clue. *Bargain:* mirrors trade reflections for memories. *Insane:* breaking a random mirror frees its moment forever (the owner, wherever they are, feels it return) — and triggers m-m1.
**Major Battle A (FAC-A): the Understudy** — triggered by the covered mirror, the catalog theft, or on exit.
**Exits:** → M2.

### M2 · The Restoration Room *(midpoint · LEVEL 3 milestone)*
**Open:** *"Behind the gallery: a workshop. Faces in clamps. Smiles being polished. A laugh, disassembled on velvet, each note pinned like a butterfly."*
**Truth:** The disassembled laugh is the mentor's. The Curator (Master Vetrine) works here — courteous, proud, genuinely believes preservation is love: *"Out there, moments END, traveler. In here, nothing is ever lost."* The campaign's thesis, spoken by the villain.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* he defends with the room (FAC-B early — harder here; warn with the environment). *Talk:* he'll show Billy the pending-acquisition file: Billy's own smile, priced. The price: "nothing you'll miss — the moments you smile when no one is watching." *Sneak:* reassembling the laugh (DC 13 group effort, three different skills — let them propose which `// VERIFY house DC`) frees it: **it flies home into Billy's spellbook-chain flask — the fragment.** *Bargain:* ⚖ Vetrine trades the laugh for "one hour of the party at their very best, to keep" (they lose the memory of their finest hour tonight; the Court gets a copy). *Insane:* wearing a clamped face: one scene as someone else entirely; the face remembers being worn.
**MILESTONE:** freeing the laugh (or any face) = **Level 3** — every mirror in the Hall ripples like water.
**Exits:** → M3 (invitations arrive: "OPENING NIGHT. ATTENDANCE: COMPULSORY." End Session 2.)

### M3 · Opening Night *(climax — Major Battle FAC-B)*
**Open:** *"The gallery has become a theatre. The mirrors are the audience. Every seat reflects someone the Court has bought, applauding on loop, and the stage is waiting for its final acquisition."*
See Battle Book. Win: the mentor's **journal, mid-transit,** recovered from the props crate — inside, page one: *"the flaw in any contract is a signatory who never signed"* in the mentor's hand (finale clue route 3). Loss: the Curator takes Billy's stage-smile (Billy cannot perform — his showman persona goes flat until recovered; the table will FEEL this one). Draw: the show is "postponed"; the journal ships onward to the Market (Act 2 chase).

## ROAD C — THE UNDER-SEA

### U1 · The Stairs Below
**Open:** *"The stairs go down into water that doesn't ask you to hold your breath. It asks you to trust it. Peaches doesn't have to be asked at all."*
**Truth:** The water lets you breathe if you were ever owed a life below `// VERIFY house`. Peaches breathes free; the rest feel a toll: one small truth spoken aloud into the water per traveler.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* Minors u-m1/u-m2/u-m3. *Talk:* drowned commuters gossip: auction tonight; "the tail lot," they say, not looking at Peaches. *Sneak:* the back-current route past Market Security (group Stealth vs the patrol — fail = MAR-A starts with the party surrounded). *Bargain:* a lantern-jelly seller rents glows "for a hum" — a literal note of music each; Peaches' hum makes the jelly burst into the missing note's harmony. Everyone nearby stares. *Insane:* Peaches announcing herself: skip to U3 immediately, on the Court's terms (doable! chaotic! the auction becomes about her).
**Major Battle A (MAR-A): Market Security** — patrol escort trying to remove them.
**Exits:** → U2.

### U2 · The Collector's Window *(midpoint · LEVEL 3 milestone)*
**Open:** *"The shop window is the size of a cathedral door. In it, on black velvet, lit by patient blue light: a mermaid's tail. The card reads: PAID IN FULL — ONE SONG."*
**Truth:** Salt-Mother Naretha (auctioneer; Sea Hag stats when needed `// VERIFY SRD`) holds the tail for tonight's auction "on behalf of the Below." The sale-ledger line — *"one song, already paid"* — is the finale-flaw clue, route 1: the song paid for the tail was never the Sea's alone to spend.
**Five doors:** *Fight:* glass like a guillotine; the window fights back (Water Weird u-m3 lives in it). *Talk:* Naretha, delighted by Peaches: "The lot, come to view itself! We'll double the attendance." She'll answer three questions honestly — hags love a technicality — if Peaches agrees to APPEAR at the auction (not be sold; appear). *Sneak:* the consignment papers: the seller of the tail is signed "THE SEA (BY PROXY)". Proxy sales require the proxy's seal — and the seal is a blank space. **The first bargain is flawed and here is the paper that proves it.** *Bargain:* ⚖ buy back "one scale, for sentiment" — Naretha sells it smiling ("a free sample sells the vintage"): **the scale is the fragment.** *Insane:* smash-and-grab before the auction: U3 begins immediately, alarm variant.
**MILESTONE:** voiding, altering, or exposing any sale (even the one scale re-purchased under a clever clause) = **Level 3** — every price-card in the market flickers, uncertain, for one breath.
**Exits:** → U3 (the auction bell. End Session 2.)

### U3 · The Auction of the Tail *(climax — Major Battle SEA-B)*
**Open:** *"The auction floor is an amphitheater of shells. The audience breathes water and wears pearls that used to be wishes. Lot the first: one mermaid's tail, provenance impeccable."*
See Battle Book. Win: **the tail's consignment papers** (with the blank proxy-seal — the campaign's smoking gun) and, if they're bold, the tail itself — *but* reattaching it needs the song made whole (Act 2–3 engine; the tail travels with them, wrapped, waiting — decide with Peaches' player what that MEANS to her). Loss: the tail sells to the Buyer, glimpsed at last (silhouette, paperwork, polite nod to Peaches) — Act 2 becomes the pursuit. Draw: auction suspended "pending provenance review" — Naretha, furious and gleeful, becomes an unlikely ally (hags LOVE a contested contract).

# PART III — THE BATTLE BOOK

**The fifth chair (party is now FIVE):** every budget below was mathed for four adventurers. With five, XP thresholds rise ~25% (L1 Moderate 300→375, L2 Moderate 600→750, L3 Moderate 900→1,125 `// VERIFY DMG 2024 thresholds`) and — more importantly — the party gets five turns to the foes' few. Standing adjustment, one line per battle:

- **CARN-A:** +1 Flying Sword (350 XP).
- **BOG-A:** +2 Awakened Shrub (490 XP — stays gentle; the Tree just has more voices to hush).
- **BOG-B:** +1 Zombie (850 XP; the barge clock matters more than the bodies).
- **FAC-A:** +1 Flying Sword (350 XP; the Understudy now mimics TWO of Billy's spells per round of theatre).
- **FAC-B:** +1 Shadow (1,000 XP).
- **MAR-A:** +1 Merfolk Skirmisher (still deliberately easy — it is an escort).
- **SEA-B:** +1 Merfolk Skirmisher (~725–925 XP; the real fight is procedural anyway).

If a fight is running away from you either direction, the retreat condition is your throttle — pull it early, never apologize. Minor battles need no adjustment; they are seasoning.

**Standing rules for every battle:** foes take, delay, or repossess — never murder. Every battle lists its retreat condition; use it. A downed PC at 0 HP is offered a bargain by the nearest foe (see What-If 10). **The alligator** is present in every major battle: it warns (one round early, always), drags one drowning/dying PC to safety once per battle, and stares at the water when the Court is near. It cannot be killed; it can be *taken* — only by paperwork, and that's an Act 2 plot, not a combat outcome.

### CARN-A · "The Empty Costumes" (carnival, optional — L1, must be gentle)
2× Flying Sword (¼, 50) + 1× Animated Armor (1, 200) = **300 XP vs 300 Moderate budget** `// VERIFY`. Repossession porters — carnival costumes with nobody inside — collecting the prize stall's overdue stock. **Arena** (stage map: the midway): prize shelves (climbable, collapsible), a ring-toss stall (improvised nets), lantern strings (cut = darkness 10-ft patches). **Tricks:** costumes ignore anyone not holding stall property; dropping the loot ends their interest instantly (teach the lesson: the Court only wants what's "theirs"); the Armor folds flat and slides under stalls. **Goal:** carry crates to the back fence. **Retreat:** stock secured or armor destroyed. **Win:** the stallkeep gifts Billy the page freely + a discount forever. **Loss:** the crates (and Billy's page) go BELOW — recoverable at the Market (cross-road hook). **Draw:** half the stock gone; the stallkeep whispers the word "tithe" for the first time.

### BOG-A · "The Hedge That Hushes" (B1→B2 door — L2)
1× Awakened Tree (2, 450) + 2× Awakened Shrub (0, 10) = **470 XP vs 600 Moderate L2** `// VERIFY`. Cassia's topiary doorman demands a toll: one voice, temporarily. **Arena:** the causeway's end — 5-ft-wide planks, black water both sides, hanging jars (throwable; each freed voice SCREAMS, deafening the Tree for a round). **Tricks:** the Tree hushes loud PCs first (grapple priority = whoever talks most); fire terrifies it into parley; the shrubs are cowards. **Goal:** take a toll, not lives. **Retreat:** toll paid, or burned twice. **Win:** the door opens; Cassia respects force ("you'll do"). **Loss:** it takes a PC's voice as toll (recoverable inside — jar #1 on the left, Cassia will trade it back cheap; she thinks the Hedge overcharges). **Parley:** singing to it works — it's starving for music.

### BOG-B · "The Tithe Barge" (B3 climax — L3 setpiece)
1× Water Weird (3, 700) bound to the barge + 2× Zombie (¼, 50 — drowned debtors, tragic, slow) = **800 XP vs 900 Moderate L3** `// VERIFY`. The Ferryman of Receipts (no stats; the coat poling the barge — attack it and it simply hands the attacker a receipt for one dignity) oversees collection. **Arena** (stage map: dock + barge): crate stacks (humming, topple-able), the tithe bell (ringing it early confuses the Weird — one round stunned `// VERIFY house`), lantern-buoys (light anchors the Weird's reach). **Tricks:** the Weird cannot leave the barge's shadow; crates broken open free voices that distract the zombies (they remember being people); the manifest hangs on the mast, in plain sight, laminated in wax. **Goal:** load Cassia's tithe and depart on schedule. **Retreat:** ON SCHEDULE — the barge leaves at the top of the hour regardless; the fight has a visible clock (put a timer on the iPad). **Win/Loss/Draw:** see B3.

### FAC-A · "The Understudy" (M1 — L2)
1× Specter (1, 200 — reskin: *a performance with no performer*, Billy's shape, gestures perfect, face absent) + 2× Flying Sword (¼, 50 — dueling-prop rapiers) = **300 XP vs 400–600 L2 Low–Moderate** `// VERIFY`. **Arena:** the mirror gallery — every mirror shows the fight as it *should* look (one round of delay: mirrors show attacks a beat early — the observant gain advantage once each `// VERIFY house`). **Tricks:** the Understudy mimics Billy's REAL spells (whatever he's cast tonight — keep your dice feed open and quote it); it cannot mimic anything sincere — a genuinely honest sentence from Billy staggers it (once per honest thing, and the table decides what's honest, which is the point). **Goal:** replace Billy for opening night. **Retreat:** if applauded — clap for it and it bows, helpless, until dragged offstage. **Win:** its script drops: stage directions for "OPENING NIGHT" (free intel on M3's arena). **Loss:** it walks out wearing Billy's role; Billy is "understudied" — NPCs briefly mistake the double for him at the worst moments until M3.

### FAC-B · "Opening Night" (M3 climax — L3 setpiece)
Master Vetrine, the Curator (Green Hag stats, 3, 700 `// VERIFY SRD`) + 2× Shadow (½, 100) = **900 XP vs 900 Moderate L3** `// VERIFY`. **Arena** (stage map: theatre of mirrors): the audience-mirrors (breakable — each broken mirror removes one of Vetrine's mislead-escape routes AND frees a moment that cheers the party: real, audible morale; rule: each PC's first mirror-break gives them Inspiration `// VERIFY house`), the chandelier of faces (lowerable), the props crate (the journal — grabbing it is an action and Vetrine's whole attention). **Tricks:** Vetrine narrates the fight as a review, aloud (do the voice); Invisible Passage between mirrors; the Shadows are stage-hands who only attack anyone touching Court property. **Goal:** complete the acquisition (Billy's smile) with signed consent — he offers the bargain MID-FIGHT, generously, when Billy is at his lowest. **Retreat:** if his catalog is threatened with ink (unforgivable vandalism), he parleys instantly. **Win/Loss/Draw:** see M3.

### MAR-A · "Market Security" (U1 — L2)
4× Merfolk Skirmisher (⅛, 25… `// VERIFY SRD 5.2 merfolk CR/XP`) + 1× Reef Shark (½, 100) on a leash = **~200–300 XP vs 400–600 L2 Low** `// VERIFY` — deliberately easy: it's an ESCORT, not an execution. **Arena:** market thoroughfare — stall awnings (collapsible curtains of kelp), the pressure-bubble lane (shoving a foe in pops them upward a level), crowds (drowned shoppers who tut). **Tricks:** security wants them *escorted out*, and grapples > damage; causing a scene draws Naretha's attention (skip to her intervening — she finds the party interesting, which is worse). **Goal:** remove. **Retreat:** whistle recall the moment anyone's actually dying. **Win:** confiscated gear returned with a receipt and an apology. **Loss:** escorted to the stairs; re-entry requires a disguise or Naretha's invitation (social re-entry — more fun anyway).

### SEA-B · "The Auction of the Tail" (U3 climax — L3 setpiece)
Salt-Mother Naretha (Sea Hag, 2, 450 `// VERIFY SRD`) + 2× Merfolk Skirmisher (⅛, ~25) + 1× Swarm of Quippers (1, 200) = **~700–900 XP vs 900 Moderate L3** `// VERIFY`. **Arena** (stage map: shell amphitheater): the auction block (the tail, in its case — the case answers only to the gavel), the gavel (an artifact of "SOLD" — whoever holds it can *close or void one lot*; getting it is the real objective), pressure bubbles (vertical movement), the audience (never fights; bids ON THE COMBAT — "200 pearls on the little one with the book!"). **Tricks:** Naretha's Horrific Appearance is her opening bid; killing her BREAKS the consignment (the tail defaults to the Court — worst outcome; the fight's secret rule is that she must be beaten *procedurally*: gavel, papers, or out-bid); Peaches singing the missing note makes the tail glow in its case and the whole audience forgets to breathe — one full round of stunned silence, once `// VERIFY house`. **Goal:** complete the sale. **Retreat:** "the Below does not brawl over settled lots" — any legal challenge (the blank proxy-seal!) suspends everything. **Win/Loss/Draw:** see U3.

# PART V — THE WHAT-IF APPENDIX

1. **They attack the Twins** — no combat; the lanterns dim; Hum stops humming (the silence physically hurts, 1 psychic to all `// VERIFY house`); Hush writes: "THAT COSTS EXTRA." The toll doubles: two honest answers each. The carnival never forgets — stall prices double all night.
2. **They refuse all three gates** — the carnival begins packing itself up around them, politely, at terrifying speed. By dawn there is bare sand, the Twins, three gates free-standing, and the tide coming BACK. The gates are the only dry exits.
3. **They split across two gates** — allowed. The gates deposit both groups in the SAME road's scene 1 from different sides (the roads share a border in the Feywild's geometry): "You went different ways and arrived at each other." Never actually split the table.
4. **They steal the carousel organ** — CARN-A triggers with double costumes; the organ WANTS to go (it slides toward Peaches); if they get it out, they've carried the Court's transcription machine into their camp: it plays their dreams at night. Amazing. Let them keep it.
5. **Billy signs something terrible immediately** — let him. The quill writes it, the ledger holds it, and Act 2 gains a personal repossession subplot. Never protect them from consented ink; that's the theme.
6. **Peaches tells everyone the truth loudly** — the carnival applauds: "WHAT a bit!" Grey-Gill pales. That night's whisper to Peaches: *"Some truths are collateral. Ask the old woman what she still owes."*
7. **They befriend the Buyer** — the Buyer is friendly! Courteous, complimentary, leaves a card: "FOR WHEN YOU HAVE SOMETHING WORTH SELLING." Anyone who keeps the card: their Bell With No Tongue never rings for the Buyer again (they've consented to contact).
8. **They try to leave the way they came** — the tide is back in. Grey-Gill, quietly: "The carnival only docks on the way OUT, dears. Going home is what the song was FOR." First hint that ending the campaign = restoring the song.
9. **A player is absent** — their character "walks with the pixies": faded to translucence, present but fictionally elsewhere; their secrets stay sealed; the alligator sleeps on their chair.
10. **A PC dies anyway** — death is a NEGOTIATION: time stops, the nearest Court agent (there is always one) offers the dying a contract — return, for a price named later (⚖ real contract, "price: one thing of Ours to choose, when We choose"). Refusal is allowed and means death is real — honor it, and the Reliquary keeps their fragment burning as the party's cause. Never undo a refusal.
11. **They gamble everything at the midway** — let the scrip flow; the house always wins EXCEPT the Derby (the snails are honest — it's why the carnival keeps them).
12. **They try to free Grey-Gill's name** — Act 2–3 sidequest seeded: her name is in the Court's ledger under "G". She will deny wanting it back. Her tent leaks salt water for an hour after they leave.
13. **They adopt a racing snail** — of course they do. It's named by the table, it's now on the party strip in your notes, and it is NEVER in danger. The Court does not buy snails. (No one knows why. Running theory among stallkeeps: snails carry their homes; nothing to take.)
14. **They rob the prize stall** — CARN-A triggers on THEM; the costumes politely repossess the stolen prizes plus one personal item each as "restocking fee" (recoverable at the Market).
15. **They long-rest inside the carnival** — time slips: they wake and the carnival is mid-pack-up (jump to What-If 2's pressure). One rest is free; the Feywild charges for the second.
16. **They interrogate the alligator** — it stares. It stares SO well. (It speaks exactly once in this campaign, in Act 3, and the line is: *"She sang to me first."* Save it.)
17. **They try to burn a contract** — Court paper doesn't burn; it files itself somewhere safer and adds a "handling fee." The Inkless Quill and the blank proxy-seal are the only weapons that work on paper — teach this early so Act 3's solution feels earned.
18. **They try the gates one by one** — the first gate crossed closes the others "until chosen last" (Act 2 revisits). Whichever they walk FIRST is the road; no takebacks; Hush's slate: "THE FEYWILD DOES NOT DO REFUNDS."
19. **They warn the other carnival-goers** — most laugh. ONE family listens, packs, and leaves… and is the only mortal thread the Court now has no paper on. In Act 3, that family matters (witnesses outside the contract). Reward sincerity with plot.
20. **They out-lawyer you with your own contract text** — THEY WIN. Standing rule: any player who finds a genuine flaw in quill-written terms gets the flaw honored, gloriously, in the fiction. The campaign's finale IS this move — let them practice.

# SESSION 1 (REVISED) — SALTMERE NIGHT · the Dry Anchor tutorial

The original Session 1 (gate → Three Gates) is now **Session 2**, unchanged.
Session 1 is a zero-death tutorial night inside Saltmere, ending on the tide
cliffhanger. Beats: **Talent Night at the Dry Anchor → The Brawl ✦ tutorial →
The Tide Goes Out.**

## The flow

1. **Cold open:** everyone is already IN the tavern, on the bill for Talent
   Night. First table moment: Maddy Brine collects every weapon by name —
   "weapons sleep behind the bar" — and each player introduces their
   character as she takes theirs.
2. **The talent show:** each player describes their act, then makes ONE check
   with any skill they can justify, DC 10 `// VERIFY house`. Success = the
   room is theirs. Failure = a beautiful disaster, cheered anyway. The show
   is spotlight, not gatekeeping — everyone wins Talent Night.
   The Appraiser writes after each act. When Peaches performs, send her
   whisper (the water stops to listen) — her song is WHY the tide leaves.
3. **The brawl** (below).
4. **The cliffhanger:** outside, the sea is gone; carnival lights a mile out
   on the sand. END ON THE LIGHTS.

## S1-BRAWL · "The Dry Anchor Classic" (L1, zero-death teaching fight)

**THE ONE RULE (house, announce it proudly):** tonight, every hit is a
knockout hit. ANY creature reduced to 0 HP — players included — is
unconscious and stable: no death saves, no dying, wake at dawn with a
headache and a story. (Grounded in the 2024 "knocking a creature out on a
melee hit" option `// VERIFY PHB 2024`, extended tonight to all brawl
damage as an explicit house rule so being KO'd is genuinely possible and
genuinely safe.)

**No blades:** their weapons are behind the bar. Unarmed strikes: 1 + STR
modifier bludgeoning; a hit can instead Grapple or Shove `// VERIFY PHB
2024 Unarmed Strike`. Improvised weapons: 1d4 + modifier `// VERIFY`.

**The opposition** (waves — pace to the table):
- **Wave 1:** 4× Brawling Local — Commoner `// VERIFY SRD: AC 10, HP 4,
  +2 to hit, 1d4 fists`. They telegraph, they miss, they fall over tables.
- **Wave 2:** 4 more, plus flying stew.
- **"Boss":** **Old Griff**, harbormaster — Bandit stats reskinned to bare
  knuckles `// VERIFY SRD: AC 12, HP 11, +3 to hit, 1d4+1` — wading in to
  "restore order" by participating enthusiastically.
- **Never fighting:** Maddy (bar sovereignty), the Appraiser (watching).

**Teaching waves:** R1 initiative + attack rolls vs AC · R2 Shove/Grapple +
improvised weapons · R3 advantage, Help action, the environment · R4 Griff +
the chandelier moment. Call out each new tool as the round starts.

**If a PC goes down:** narrate stars, distant fiddle music, and waking up
propped in a chair with Maddy applying a cold ham to their eye. Being KO'd
in the Dry Anchor Classic is a badge of honor — Old Griff respects them
MORE.

**Aftermath:** no XP math — milestone leveling stands (Level 2 remains the
gate crossing, Session 2). Maddy returns the weapons with one dry comment
each. Then the door, the cold air, and the sea that isn't there.
