# SPEC ADDENDUM — Best-in-Class Magic Layer
### Extends `campaign-companion-build-spec.md`. Phases 1–3 remain the foundation and ship first. Everything below is organized by magic-per-effort, not by category.

Guiding principle: **the app should feel like an artifact FROM the Feywild, not software ABOUT it.** Every feature below must pass one test — does it make the table lean in?

Second principle, equally binding — **the Three Protagonists Rule:** this campaign has three main characters, not one main character and two friends. The mermaid's lore is simply the lore we happen to know first, because her player built first. Every personal-story feature below must be a *system with three slots*, never a mermaid feature with add-ons. The other two slots stay as clearly-labeled placeholders until those players create their characters — their quiz answers (what they'd risk everything to recover, what they fear losing) are the seed material, and their features get the same craft budget the mermaid's do.

---

## TIER A — Highest magic per effort (build these right after Phase 2)

**A1. The Bargain Ledger.**
Feywild deals are the heart of this campaign. Any time a PC strikes a bargain (with a hag, a pixie, the carnival itself), the DM issues it as an illuminated contract on that player's phone: ornate border, the terms in fairy-tale legalese, a wax seal, and a "sign" interaction (finger-drawn signature). The ledger tab shows all active bargains and — crucially — what remains *owed*. Breaking or fulfilling a bargain triggers a visual (seal cracks / contract burns gold). This turns the campaign's core theme into a physical ritual players will remember forever.

**A2. Sealed Whispers.**
Upgrade the Phase 3 secret handout: it arrives as a sealed envelope on one player's phone. They physically swipe to break the wax. Typewriter text reveal. Optional "this message ends in 60 seconds" mode for dreams and whispers the character can't hold onto. The Druid's alligator clues use this channel.

**A3. The Reliquary (the endgame engine — three tracks, always).**
A collectible system with one track per character. As the party recovers pieces of each PC's stolen quality, fragments appear in that character's Reliquary as illustrated cards, each with a sensory motif. The mermaid's track is her song — each fragment adds an instrument/voice layer to one melody, complete only at the finale. The other two tracks are architected identically but stay as named placeholders (`Track 2`, `Track 3`) until those characters exist; their motifs get chosen from what those players lost (a stolen laugh could be sound, a stolen name could be letters assembling, a stolen shadow could be an image un-fading — decided with the DM after character creation). The finale moment combines all three completed reliquaries, not just the song. Build the system so all three tracks are first-class citizens; the mermaid's is merely the first one populated.

**A4. Condition & concentration co-pilot.**
When the DM applies a condition, it appears on the player's sheet in plain language ("Frightened: roll with disadvantage while you can see the wolf; you can't willingly move closer to it"). When a concentrating caster takes damage, their phone automatically prompts: "Concentration check — DC 10, tap to roll." Removes the #1 source of forgotten rules at real tables.

**A5. The table roll feed.**
All dice rolls (from the tappable sheet dice) stream to a shared feed on the iPad with the character's name and what they rolled for. Natural 20s get a lantern-burst; natural 1s get a sad little carnival trombone flourish. DM rolls can be public or hidden per-roll. Shared dice drama is half the fun of D&D — put it on the big screen.

## TIER B — Maps & the world made visible

**B1. Map engine upgrades (still not a VTT).**
Keep the "picture with movable pieces" philosophy, add: (a) tap-to-reveal region masks the DM paints in prep mode — simple fog, no line-of-sight math; (b) pinch-zoom with token scale locking; (c) player map pins — any player can drop a note-pin ("the frog statue was HERE"), building a communal memory of Prismeer; (d) a DM "ping" that ripples on all screens.

**B2. Journey mode.**
Between locations, the iPad shows the Prismeer overview map with an animated dotted line crawling Indiana-Jones-style to the destination while the DM narrates. Cheap to build, enormous atmosphere.

**B3. Asset licensing guardrail (non-negotiable).**
The app must NOT ship or embed Wizards of the Coast copyrighted maps, art, or adventure text. Instead: (a) a private upload slot where the owner adds scans/photos from his purchased book for his own table's use; (b) built-in support for free community/CC-licensed Feywild maps; (c) monster stat quick-reference cards drawn ONLY from SRD 5.2 (Creative Commons) content. Flag any gray area to the owner instead of guessing.

## TIER C — Session-night joy

**C1. Carnival minigames.**
Real tappable minigames for Session 1's carnival, played on phones in ~60 seconds each, results feeding the DM: a giant-snail race (players tap-tap-tap to sprint their snail — the Witchlight Carnival famously has one), a fortune-wheel, and a "catch the pixie" reflex game. Winning earns in-fiction carnival prizes the DM has pre-loaded. Players remember Session 1 forever.

**C2. Scene moods.**
DM taps a scene (Carnival / Hither's bogs / Thither's woods / Yon's peaks / Dream) and the whole app subtly re-skins for everyone — palette shift, ambient background, iPad soundscape (looped royalty-free audio; keep files small). The world changes and everyone's phone changes with it.

**C3. Tethers — every character gets a haunted companion element.**
A "tether" is a small living element on a character's sheet that carries a piece of their mystery. The Druid's tether is an animated alligator sprite: it idles, blinks, does comedic bits, and the DM has a hidden trigger panel ("stare at the water," "hum a fragment of song," "refuse to enter," "fall asleep mid-scene") that fires only on her phone. Build tethers as a system with three slots. The other two are designed after character creation from each player's concept and quiz answers — a tether could be a sprite (a moth that lands on certain words), an object (a pocket watch that runs backward near lies), or an ambient effect (their sheet's shadow moving when it shouldn't). Same trigger-panel architecture for all three; the DM haunts each player equally.

**C4. Heroic Inspiration coin.**
A physical-feeling golden ticket on the sheet. DM grants it with a shimmer; spending it is a satisfying flip animation. One banked at a time, per the rules.

**C5. Level-up ceremony.**
When the DM grants a milestone, players get a full-screen moment ("The Feywild has taken notice of you") and a guided level-up wizard — new features explained in plain language, choices validated, sheet recalculated. Requires the level 2–8 class data; populate progressively, one level ahead of the party.

## TIER D — Memory & keepsakes (cheap, high sentiment)

**D1. Quote wall.** Anyone can tap-save a line said at the table. Displayed as carnival posters. End-of-campaign, this is gold.
**D2. "Previously on…"** Session-start recap screen the DM writes from a template (3 bullets max), styled as a storybook page. (Future: AI-drafted; not v1.)
**D3. Whimsical achievements.** "Befriended something with too many teeth." "Made a bargain you definitely read carefully." Auto- and DM-granted.
**D4. The Epilogue Page.** At campaign end, a generated keepsake: each character's portrait slot, their arc, their recovered Lost Thing, all three completed reliquaries, the quote wall. Printable. This is the thing they screenshot and keep.
**D5. Session pulse.** End of session, one tap per player: ⭐ favorite moment (choose from tonight's quote wall) + a private "more of / less of" note to the DM. Feeds a first-time DM exactly what he needs to improve.

## TIER E — DM power tools (build only as the campaign demands)

**E1. Encounter tuner.** Enter an encounter's monsters; get 3-player-party balance guidance (action economy warning, adjust-on-the-fly suggestions per the DM's balancing checklist). SRD stat blocks only.
**E2. Feywild flourish generator.** Random tables (original content): carnival oddities, pixie mischief, bog omens, "what the alligator is staring at." One tap when the DM needs 10 seconds of wonder.
**E3. Clock & time-quirk tracker.** Witchlight plays with time (hours owed, eight-year cycles). A simple named-clocks widget ("Mister Light's hourglass: 3/8 segments").
**E4. Absent-player mode.** Mark a player absent; their character auto-fades to "wandered off with the pixies" status; their secrets stay sealed.

## Revised phase order

1. Phase 1 — Player Companion (unchanged, ships first)
2. Phase 2 — DM Dashboard (unchanged)
3. Phase 3 — Table Mode core (initiative, handouts, basic map)
4. **Phase 4 — Tier A** (Bargain Ledger, Sealed Whispers, Song Fragments, condition co-pilot, roll feed)
5. **Phase 5 — Tier B + C1/C2** (map upgrades, journey mode, minigames, scene moods)
6. **Phase 6 — remaining C + D** (alligator sprite, inspiration, level-up ceremony, keepsakes)
7. **Phase 7 — Tier E** as real play reveals need

**Hard rule:** Session 1 needs Phases 1–3 plus, if time allows, C1 (minigames) and A2 (sealed whispers) — those two punch far above their weight on night one. Everything else can arrive between sessions; a campaign is 30+ sessions long and features landing mid-campaign feel like the Feywild itself is growing.

**Audio note:** all music/soundscapes must be royalty-free or original. The song fragments (A3) should be a simple original melody built in layers — ask the owner; he produces music in GarageBand and may want to compose the mermaid's song himself.
